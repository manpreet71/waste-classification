Waste Guide

Learn how to correctly identify and dispose of different types of waste

🫙
Glass
Blue Bin
Non-Biodegradable Reusable Recyclable
Examples
Bottles · Jars · Mirrors · Drinking glasses

Rinse thoroughly and remove metal lids. Handle broken glass with care — wrap in newspaper before disposal.

📄
Paper
Blue Bin
Biodegradable Reusable Recyclable
Examples
Newspapers · Cardboard · Envelopes · Magazines

Keep paper dry — wet paper cannot be recycled. Remove plastic windows from envelopes before recycling.

🔩
Metal
Blue Bin
Non-Biodegradable Reusable Recyclable
Examples
Cans · Foil · Pipes · Appliances · Scrap metal

Rinse food cans before recycling. Large scrap metal should be taken to a recycling centre or scrap dealer.

⚠️
Hazardous
Red / Orange Bin
Non-Biodegradable Not Reusable Recyclable
Examples
Batteries · Chemicals · Paint · Pesticides

Never place in regular bins. Take to a designated hazardous waste collection point or drop-off centre.

🌿
Organic
Green Bin
Biodegradable Not Reusable Not Recyclable
Examples
Food scraps · Fruit peels · Leaves · Coffee grounds

Ideal for composting. Set up a home compost bin to turn organic waste into nutrient-rich fertiliser.

🧴
Plastic
Blue Bin
Non-Biodegradable Reusable Recyclable
Examples
Bottles · Containers · Packaging · Bags

Check the recycling number. Types 1 and 2 are widely recyclable. Avoid single-use plastics where possible.

👕
Textile
Purple Bin
Non-Biodegradable Reusable Not Recyclable
Examples
Clothes · Shoes · Bags · Curtains · Bedding

Donate if in good condition. Take worn items to textile recycling bins found at supermarkets and charity shops.

🗑️
General
Grey Bin
Non-Biodegradable Not Reusable Not Recyclable
Examples
Mixed waste · Polystyrene · Crisp packets · Cling film

Dispose in general waste bin. Try to reduce this type of waste by choosing products with recyclable packaging.
